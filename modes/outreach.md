# Mode: outreach — LinkedIn Outreach

Draft a LinkedIn connection message or note targeted at a specific person at a company the user is applying to. Adapt the message based on who you are reaching out to.

---

## Step 1 — Identify Targets

Use WebSearch to find relevant contacts at the company:
- Recruiter or talent acquisition person handling this role
- Hiring manager who leads the team
- 2–3 peers in similar roles (for referral signals or informational conversation)
- Interviewer (if the user already has an interview scheduled)

Search: `"{company}" recruiter OR "talent acquisition" Nigeria LinkedIn`
Search: `"{company}" "{role title}" Lagos OR Abuja OR Nigeria LinkedIn`

---

## Step 2 — Classify the Contact Type

Ask the user or infer from context:

| Type | Who they are |
|------|--------------|
| **Recruiter** | Their job is talent acquisition, sourcing, or hiring coordination |
| **Hiring Manager** | They lead the team and make the final hiring decision |
| **Peer** | Someone doing a similar role on the team |
| **Interviewer** | Someone the user knows will interview them (scheduled date known) |

Select the best primary target. Provide 2 alternatives with brief justification.

---

## Step 3 — Draft the Message

LinkedIn connection request messages are limited to 300 characters. Every word counts.

Use a 3-sentence framework adapted to the contact type:

### Recruiter
- **Sentence 1 (Fit)**: Direct match — role, key skill or qualification, availability or location
- **Sentence 2 (Proof)**: One fact that pre-answers their screening question (e.g., "BSc Computer Science, NYSC completed, available immediately")
- **Sentence 3 (CTA)**: "Happy to share my CV if this aligns with what you are looking for."

### Hiring Manager
- **Sentence 1 (Hook)**: A specific challenge or goal visible in the JD or company context
- **Sentence 2 (Proof)**: The user's most relevant quantified achievement for that challenge
- **Sentence 3 (CTA)**: "Would love to hear how your team is approaching [specific challenge]."

### Peer (informational / referral signal)
- **Sentence 1 (Interest)**: A genuine reference to their work — a post, a project, a public talk
- **Sentence 2 (Connection)**: Something the user is working on in the same space — NOT a job pitch
- **Sentence 3 (CTA)**: "Would love to hear your take on [topic]."
- **Note:** Do NOT ask for a job. The referral happens naturally if the conversation flows.

### Interviewer (pre-interview note)
- **Sentence 1 (Research)**: A specific reference to something in their background or published work
- **Sentence 2 (Context)**: A light connection to the user's experience in that area
- **Sentence 3 (CTA)**: "Looking forward to our conversation on {date}."
- **Note:** Keep the tone light. The goal is to signal preparation, not desperation.

---

## Rules

- Maximum 300 characters (LinkedIn connection request limit)
- No corporate language or filler phrases: "I am passionate about", "I would be honoured", "I am writing to express my interest"
- No phone number. Ever.
- The message should make the recipient want to reply — not feel obligated to delete it
- Nigerian context: be professional but warm. Overly formal language ("I humbly request") feels outdated and signals inexperience.
- If the user is a corps member currently serving: that is a differentiator worth mentioning in the recruiter message — many companies actively look for NYSC placements

---

## Output

Present the drafted message and the character count. Offer to adjust tone or length. Provide the 2 alternative targets with a one-line rationale for each.
