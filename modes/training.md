# Mode: training — Course and Certification Evaluation

Evaluate whether a course, certification, or training programme is worth pursuing at this point in the user's job search.

---

## Scoring

Rate the training across 6 dimensions:

| Dimension | What it measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| North Star alignment | Does it move the user closer to their target roles, or is it a distraction? |
| Recruiter signal | What do Nigerian hiring managers actually think when they see this on a CV? |
| Time and effort | Total weeks × hours per week — is this realistic alongside a job search? |
| Opportunity cost | What else could the user be doing with that time? (applying, building a project, networking) |
| Risk factors | Is the content outdated? Is the brand respected in Nigeria? Is it too basic to add value? |
| Portfolio output | Does completing it produce a demonstrable artefact — a project, a certificate scan, a GitHub repo? |

---

## Verdicts

**DO IT** — Worth the time. Provide a 4–12 week plan with weekly milestones and a final artefact.

**SKIP** — Not worth it at this stage. Explain why and suggest a better alternative.

**DO IT WITH A TIMEBOX** — The content has value but doesn't need to be completed in full. Define the maximum time to spend (e.g., 3 weeks) and what to focus on.

---

## Priority Framework for Nigerian Job Seekers

When recommending training, prioritise in this order:

1. **Certifications recognised by Nigerian employers** — ICAN, CIPM, COREN, NSE, ACCA, CFA for finance/professional roles; Google, AWS, Microsoft, Cisco for tech roles
2. **Practical skills that show in work output** — SQL, Python, Excel (Power Query, Power BI), AutoCAD, project management tools
3. **Internationally recognised platforms with verifiable credentials** — Coursera (with certificate), edX, professional body exams
4. **Self-paced courses that produce a project** — only if the project is demonstrable
5. **YouTube or informal learning** — valid for skill acquisition but produces no CV signal; recommend supplementing with a project

**Certifications with strong Nigerian employer recognition:**
- ICAN / ACCA — essential for accounting and finance roles
- CIPM — HR and people management roles
- NSE / COREN — engineering roles (often required for senior positions)
- CFA Level 1 — investment banking, asset management
- Google Career Certificates — recognised at tech-forward Nigerian companies
- AWS Cloud Practitioner / Associate — growing recognition at Nigerian tech firms
- Microsoft certifications (AZ-900, PL-900) — enterprise and banking sector
- Cisco CCNA — telecoms and networking roles

---

## Output Format

Present the scoring table, then the verdict in bold, then the plan (if BUILD or DO WITH TIMEBOX). Keep it direct — the user needs a clear answer, not a long essay.
